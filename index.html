Grocery shopping for the weekEveryoneFoodie

Kensington Market is one of Toronto's best grocery neighborhoods. Plan to hit a few small specialty shops for produce, meat, and pantry, then a larger store for staples. All within walking distance of the Airbnb.

Kensington specialty (closest, walking distance):
• Sanagan's Meat Locker (176 Baldwin) — premium butcher, charcuterie, prepared foods. Mon–Sat 10–7, Sun 10–6.
• Global Cheese (76 Kensington Ave) — 250+ cheeses, friendly staff, samples.
• Casa Acoreana (235 Augusta) — bulk nuts, dried fruit, spices, coffee beans.
• Perola's Supermarket (247 Augusta) — Latin American groceries, fresh produce, masa.
• Multiple fresh produce stands on Augusta and Baldwin.

Larger grocery stores (5–10 min drive or 15-min walk):
• Loblaws Maple Leaf Gardens (60 Carlton) — Toronto's flagship, fully accessible. ~10-min drive.
• No Frills Bathurst & Dundas (110 Bathurst) — closest big-box, budget. ~5-min drive.
• T&T Supermarket College Park — Asian groceries, ~10-min drive.
• Hua Sheng Supermarket (293 Spadina) — Asian groceries, walking distance from Chinatown route.

Tip: Send 2 people in one car for the big shop while others walk Kensington for fresh produce + specialty items. Reconvene at the Airbnb. Bring reusable bags — many small Kensington shops charge $0.05+ per plastic bag.

Pre-trip checklist
Toronto traffic cheat sheet
Avoid: Gardiner + DVP between 7–9 AM and 4–7 PM. Worst: Friday evenings + heavy rain.
Sat May 9 11 AM–4 PM: Messi at BMO Field = downtown gridlock. Don't drive.
Niagara day: depart by 7:30–8 AM, return after 7 PM.
TPO return Tue evening: pay 407 ETR (~$15–25) to skip 401 rush — saves 30–60 min.
Kensington ↔ CN Tower: walk or 510 Spadina streetcar (~10 min). Don't drive.
Streetcar tracks at crosswalks are the biggest hazard for wheelchairs in downtown Toronto. Cross perpendicular and slowly.
Airport runs: 60 min off-peak, 90 min in rush.
